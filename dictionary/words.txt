Abate
To reduce in intensity or amount; to lessen.
Aberration
A deviation from the normal or expected course; an anomaly.
Abhor
To regard with disgust and hatred; to detest.
Acrimony
Bitterness or ill feeling; harshness in speech or manner.
Adroit
Skillful and clever in using the hands or mind.
Altruism
The belief in or practice of selfless concern for the well-being of others.
Ambivalence
The state of having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone.
Ameliorate
To make something bad or unsatisfactory better; to improve.
Apathy
Lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
Aptitude
A natural ability to do something; talent.
Archaic
Very old or old-fashioned; belonging to an earlier time.
Ascetic
Characterized by severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence.
Assiduous
Showing great care, attention, and effort; diligent.
Audacious
Showing a willingness to take bold risks; daring and fearless.
Auspicious
Conducive to success; favorable or promising.
Avarice
Extreme greed for wealth or material gain.
Benevolent
Well-meaning and kindly; charitable.
Berate
To scold or criticize someone angrily.
Blandishment
A flattering or pleasing statement or action used to persuade someone.
Boorish
Rough and bad-mannered; coarse.
Brusque
Abrupt or offhand in speech or manner; blunt.
Cacophony
A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds.
Cajole
To persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery.
Capricious
Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior; unpredictable.
Censure
Express severe disapproval of someone or something, especially in a formal statement.
Chastise
To rebuke or reprimand severely.
Chicanery
The use of trickery to achieve a political, financial, or legal purpose.
Clandestine
Kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit.
Clemency
Mercy; lenience.
Coerce
To compel or force someone to do something by using threats or pressure.
Cogent
Clear, logical, and convincing.
Complacent
Showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.
Concur
To agree or be of the same opinion.
Condone
To accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong or offensive.
Conflagration
A large destructive fire; often used metaphorically to describe a conflict.
Conundrum
A confusing and difficult problem or question; a riddle.
Copious
Abundant in supply or quantity.
Craven
Contemptibly lacking in courage; cowardly.
Credulous
Having or showing too great a readiness to believe things; gullible.
Cryptic
Having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure.
Cursory
Hasty and therefore not thorough or detailed.
Dauntless
Showing fearlessness and determination.
Debilitate
To make someone weak and infirm; to enfeeble.
Decorum
Behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety; etiquette.
Deference
Humble submission and respect.
Demure
Reserved, modest, and shy (typically used to describe a woman).
Deprecate
To express disapproval of.
Despot
A ruler or other person who holds absolute power, typically one who exercises it in a cruel or oppressive way.
Diatribe
A forceful and bitter verbal attack against someone or something.
Dichotomy
A division or contrast between two things that are represented as being opposed or entirely different.
